Problem. 1 The required conditions follow naturally as: $d'(x,x) = \sqrt{d(x,x)} = \sqrt{0} = 0$. If $d(x,y) > 0$ then $d'(x,y) > 0$ as the square root of non-zero is non-zero. Otherwise $0^2 = 0$ contradicting the fact $d'(x,y) > 0$. $d'(x,y) = \sqrt{d(x,y)} = \sqrt{d(y,x)} = d'(y,x)$. $d'(x,y) = \sqrt{d(x,y)} \leq \sqrt{d(x,r) + d(r,y)} \leq \sqrt{d(x,r)} + \sqrt{d(r,y)} = d'(x,r) + d'(r,y)$. For an arbitrary open-set of $d$, $\set{ y | d(x,y) < r \}$ there is an equivalent open-set of $d'$, $\set{ y | d'(x,y) < \sqrt{r} \}$.
