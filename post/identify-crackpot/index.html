How to Identify a Crackpot

Make it fun, Not a heated religious debate

Nov 6, 2019

Introduction

This post is not meant to critise anyone, Nor does it aim to judge a person’s character. I just share couple of traits, some of them I had couple of years ago, by means of humor on how you miss the gist of your achievement due to a misconception, an idea or a practice.

  • Lacking Concern of Business or Society

Speaking Loudly of Achievements

Those who always speak about their brilliance, their hard work, their astronomical achievements are usually crackpots. The more deeply you understand and solve problems, the more skeptic you becomes about taking further steps, not more confident. A truly matured boy/girl realizes how hard and risky it is to kick-off a start-up or prove a theorem, so that he/she would not venture to claiming success. Putting it another way, Speaking loudly is a sign that you have not polished a matured degree of sophistication.

Lacking Concern of Business or Society

Of course, It is infeasible for all people to become business men or public-figure leaders. But even if you were a programmer or a mathematician, Unless your work had society or business in mind, hoping to have an influence on shaping modern civilzation, You would be doomed as a crackpot. Even Bertrand Russell, The great philosopher who had a great influence on mathematical logic, authored The Conquest of Happiness as a remedy to evil commonly faced in everyday life. I have met highly dedicated people who just got captivated by doing pure-science but with no interest on having impact on society. “Why would I care about business? I am just a science guy”, A common response by such people. Another one would be “I am not aiming to gain money, I wish to educate others”. Community voluntarily work is great but how are you going to gain the community’s empowerment? How far is your work significant? Those are central concerns besides your pretty philosophy of volunteering

Regarding Reading Books as Superfluous

It is common to just google a problem or an investigation instead of searching through books. While online-communities are great, They are not sufficient to polish a deep understanding. On the other hand, A published book must adhere to strict academic regulations such as providing a conclusion in order to assess whether the chapter is of interest to you; providing explainations according to scientific standards in order not to get a misleading insight; and providing references for futher study. Such characterisitcs in books are not usually found in hobbyists blogs (yes, including the blog you are reading now). As an undergrad computer science student, My college mates and even TAs and doctors, find it weird to pick-up a book. “It is unnecessary to go through all this long text. The problem could be solved by a Youtube video or an online tutorial”. A reply commonly uttered. Well, My short answer is that, Having a functioning code is not the same as understanding it.

Using Microsoft Office

You might conceive this point in particular as a weird trait of crackpots. Partially, It is attributed due to Microsoft’s over-reliance on GUI. Usually, there is a tradeoff between easeness and professionalism. The more easy it is, the more details the software hides from you. Such details hidden by easy-to-use software might be required by professional standards. Here is a personal case: TeX, the typesetting system designed by the legend Donald Knuth, is the standard of professional academic writing. Nonethless, I have seen PhD holders who write their academic papers in Microsoft Word. Probably, LaTeX is just too cumbersome for them. It turned out that in the same manner they pick-up the easy path of using Microsoft Word, they favor short-cuts in teaching. In the same manner they think of themselves as hackers just because they figured how to write a formula in Microsoft Word, They think of themselves as Richard Feynman while they are just crackpots.
